Two Different Visualizations

This project provides two interactive maps that explore housing permits and population distribution across Seattle.

Proportional Symbols Map

Each dot represents a housing permit, with symbol size determined by the scale of development. Colors distinguish whether the permit has been issued or completed.

Population Heatmap

The heatmap highlights highly populated areas throughout Seattle, allowing users to quickly identify population density patterns across the city.

About the Project

Project Overview

This project aims to provide insight into how effectively housing development responds to demographic changes. By visualizing these relationships spatially, users can better understand where housing supply may be keeping up with demand and where gaps may exist.

The broader impact includes improving transparency in housing development patterns and potentially informing city planners, policymakers, researchers, and residents.

Data Processing

We cleaned and formatted the datasets by removing irrelevant columns, handling missing data, standardizing attributes, and filtering by year. Cleaned datasets are stored in the assets/ folder.

Key Objectives

  • Increase public awareness of housing development patterns and trends in Seattle.
  • Help city planners and policymakers make data-driven decisions about housing supply and urban growth.
  • Enable residents, researchers, and community members to explore how housing permits and population growth vary across neighborhoods over time.

The interactive map includes time sliders and dropdown filters to allow users to explore the data in ways best suited to them and better assess the relationship between housing supply and population growth.

Target Audience

  • Seattle City Planners – can utilize the map for informed decision-making when planning developments.
  • Housing Policy Researchers – can assess housing trends relative to population change.
  • Students & General Public – can explore housing development patterns across neighborhoods.
