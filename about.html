About the Project

Project Overview

This project aims to provide insight into how effectively housing development responds to demographic changes. By visualizing these relationships spatially, users can better understand where housing supply may be keeping up with demand and where gaps may exist. The broader impact includes improving transparency in housing development patterns and potentially informing city planners, policymakers, researchers, and residents.

Data Processing

We cleaned and formatted the datasets by removing irrelevant columns, handling missing data, standardizing attributes, and filtering by year. Cleaned datasets are stored in the assets/ folder.

Key Objectives

  • Increase public awareness of housing development patterns and trends in Seattle.
  • Help city planners and policymakers make data-driven decisions about housing supply and urban growth.
  • Enable residents, researchers, and community members to explore how housing permits and population growth vary across neighborhoods over time.

The interactive map will feature time sliders and dropdown filters to allow users to explore the data in ways best suited to them. This will enable users to clearly see trends occuring, and assess the relationship between housing supply and population growth across the Seattle area.

The user will be able to sort by permit type, dwelling type, neighborhood, and urban village name, highlighting the key components of the data. The user will also have access to time sliders, which will allow them to see when permits were issued and completed. Adding this time component will assist users in understanding the yearly contexts that may have influenced housing development patterns at the time.

Target Audience

  • Seattle City Planners – can utilize the map for informed decision-making when planning future developments.
  • Housing Policy Researchers – can assess the data to understand housing trends in reference to population data.
  • Students & General Public – can explore the map for personal or educational purposes, getting a better idea of what is going on within different communities.
